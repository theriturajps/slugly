SEO Best Practices for URL Slugs

Follow these guidelines to create slugs that are both search engine and user friendly:

1. Keep it concise

Aim for slugs that are 3-5 words or 50-60 characters long. Shorter URLs are easier to read, share, and may perform better in search results.

2. Use relevant keywords

Include the main keywords that describe your page content. Place the most important words first.

3. Hyphenate words

Use hyphens (-) to separate words, not underscores (_). Search engines treat hyphens as word separators but group words connected by underscores.

4. Use lowercase consistently

URLs are case-sensitive, and using lowercase exclusively prevents confusion and duplicate content issues.

5. Remove unnecessary elements

Eliminate stop words (a, an, the), dates, and numbers unless they're essential to the content.

6. Make it readable

Create slugs that humans can easily read and understand at a glance. Avoid random strings of characters.

7. Avoid special characters

Stick to letters, numbers, and hyphens. Other characters may need URL encoding and make links harder to share.

8. Be consistent

Establish a pattern for your slugs and stick to it across your entire website for better user experience and SEO.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a URL slug?

A URL slug is the part of a URL that identifies a particular page on a website in a human-readable form. It typically appears after the domain name and is used for SEO and user experience purposes.

Example: In "https://example.com/blog/perfect-url-slugs", "perfect-url-slugs" is the slug.

Why should I use Slugly instead of manual slugs?

Slugly ensures your slugs follow SEO best practices consistently, handles special characters and edge cases properly, and saves you time by automating the process. It also provides multiple customization options that would be time-consuming to implement manually.

Is my data secure when using Slugly?

Absolutely! Slugly processes all text entirely in your browser. No data is sent to any servers or stored anywhere. Your content never leaves your device.

Can I use Slugly for non-English text?

Yes, Slugly handles international characters. The "Remove accents" option can convert accented characters to their ASCII equivalents for better URL compatibility.

What's the difference between hyphen and underscore separators?

Search engines treat hyphens (-) as word separators, meaning "hello-world" is interpreted as two words. Underscores (_) are treated as connectors, so "hello_world" is seen as one word. For SEO, hyphens are generally preferred.

Should I remove stop words from my slugs?

Removing stop words (a, an, the, etc.) can make slugs more concise, but consider readability. For short slugs, keeping stop words might make them more natural. For longer slugs, removing them can help focus on keywords.
